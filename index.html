How we split things — the principles

🛡️

Protected on the downside — we start at 50/50

If the company stays small, we share equally. The first $2M of lifetime profit is split 50/50, and at low exit values equity is 50/50 too. The work that goes into building this is protected no matter how it turns out.

📈

Upside follows ownership — shifting toward 80/20 as it scales

As the company grows and the founding bet proves out, the split moves toward 80/20 — the same ownership on both the exit and the profit. The bigger it gets, the more it reflects the founding split.

⬆️

Your share only ever grows — it never gets cut

Profit is measured over the whole life of the business, not year by year. So a single weak year never reduces your share, and no one can shuffle the timing of profit to anyone's disadvantage. Your cumulative take only goes up.

💵

Cash when you need it — Lior's draw

Lior's pay is his profit share — there's no separate salary holding it back. Once the business clears $100K/month in profit, Lior can take up to $10K/month in cash to live on, while Daniel takes nothing in that period. The draw is just early access to your own share; it doesn't change the split.
